My Practical Gen AI usecase at work?

During my time interviewing several candidates across various levels—from junior to director—I faced the challenge of balancing their specific seniority with their unique skill sets. I realized that rather than interviewing a candidate on their weaknesses, it was more effective to focus on the strengths highlighted in their resumes.

Since my firm provided Microsoft Copilot access, I integrated the candidates' resumes and my standard questionnaire into the tool. I began by prompting Copilot to "use the resume to pick three questions from the question bank that specifically align with this candidate's skill set."

After proofreading the initial suggestions, I would refine the prompt to better target the candidate's seniority: "Choose five questions instead, ensuring they are appropriate for a director-level role."

This approach made the interview process much smoother and helped the candidates feel more comfortable. I also ensured that the questions and skills perfectly matched the specific job roles to avoid any conflicts, which significantly reduced my overall workload.

I took the process a step further by using Copilot to rephrase my interview notes into structured feedback. I organized my evaluations into "must-haves," "areas for improvement," and "nice-to-haves." This clear structure greatly assisted HR and the hiring manager in their final decision-making process.

Describe several scenarios where using generative AI is not a good idea

Using Copilot to write code in a language the developer does not understand can be risky. Creating an entire project without a formal review from an architect or Site Reliability Engineer (SRE) can lead to significant security vulnerabilities in production.

Uploading sensitive documents to these models can create serious governance and compliance issues for an organization.

It is important to allow your brain to think and retrieve information independently. Avoid jumping straight to an AI for every instant idea, as this can lead to a dependency where you feel unable to work without it.

Since most models are trained to suggest consulting a medical professional for serious conditions, leaning entirely on an AI's medical answers could lead to disastrous results.
